Ride ride renne, fasten the golden buckles, first with kvemme, and then with kvamme, and then my little baby. No one is home except an old man, who sits in Vrå and spools blue, and asked his daughter to spin: spin, spin, daughter mine, tomorrow your suitors will come, father has gone to Nørregård, buys a little gingerbread  for the little bitty [Hanne], we want to cook those ducks, but they’ll have to wait til Easter, til Pentecost, til the barley is sown, til the oats are mowed, til the fields and meadow are in flower.
